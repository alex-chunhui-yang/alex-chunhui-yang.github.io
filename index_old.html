Drone OpenCV Flighting Control Paparazzi

MAV Competition

This event is a teamwork where I take part in as a chief programmer. The UAV we used is one of the products made by Parrot that has an academic cooperation with Delft’s MAVlab, Aerospace Department.

I firstly test the basic algorithm with OpenCV library in Python and then transplant it into a C++ code that the flight control system can recognize and successfully run. To be more specific, the flight control system is Parparazzi based on Linux framework. The goal of this event is to develop a robust algorithm to avoid obstacles inside the cyberzoo, as you can see, in order to let the drone cover as much area as possible.

The following are the sequential procedure of our algorithm:

  • Filter certain color at very beginning;
  • Classify different cluster and figure out the contour of each cluster;
  • Find the points each of which has a dropping value of variance in certain range;
  • Locate the turning point to make sure where the roots of the obstacles are;
  • Always turn right if there are points in the middle range of the image;

As a result, our UAV can recognize almost all the obstacles that have different colors including orange pillars, red windows and green ground. Luckily, we won the fourth place in this competition. :)

Automobile PID control algorithm Linear CCD

Electronic Design Contest at Tsinghua University

This event is based on a competition match held by EE department, Tsinghua University. I played a role as the team leader.

As assigned with same motors and also restricted with a maximum size, we designed our own PCB board instead of adding modules stick to the original board in order to lower down the weight and achieve a higher flexibility in velocity control. The single chip microcomputer we use is STM32F10X.

There is one innovative part, which is different from any other teams' design, in our own vehicle that is the “eyes”. Since the race map consists of only two colors, black and white, a linear CCD camera was chose to discern the boundary from the race tunnel. With a PID control algorithm, it became much intelligent in making decisions on the time when it should turn or move forward. In all, the keys to this success would be considered as a lighter weight, a robust control algorithm, a clever and much more efficient facility to detect boundaries, and the cool team.

As the result, our team won the first place, namely the outstanding prize in this competition. : )

fMRI Generative Adversirial Networks Data Recovery

Recovering loss data in fMRI

This research project was conducted during my summer intern in Liu’s lab, MGH & HST, Boston.
The basic algorithm are shown as the following:

  • DCGANs model is trained by normal control’s fMRI data with bold signal;
  • Fix the Generator, give it an expected image with certain area masked then train the input vector in order to generate a new image, which is the most similar one to the expected one but with the mask area data compensated
  • Compare the functional performance of generated image with that of the expected image then make sure that the generated one has the similar bold value.

This research can be considered to be one of the most effective ways to recover the bold data in the brain area, where the DBS device is planted.

Drama Life

Every year we host one show per semester, which are open to the public. There are two shows in which I played as a main character. Acting in these roles was invaluable to my maturing as an active member of society; in the role-playing, you have to develop empathy for your characters and their daily lives.

In Somebody Is Going to Come written by Jon Fosse, a renowned dramatist from Norway, I played the landlord role. My character had a complex emotional and physical relationship with his tenants, a couple who had moved from an urban to rural setting; this show revealed key aspects of couples’ relationships, such as suspicion caused by an outsider, paranoia, control, and uncontrollable sexual jealousy.

In the black comedy, The Insanity recomposed from the original script composed by Xiaozhi Rao, a famous young pioneer director in China, my lawyer character was eventually disclosed as having multiple personality disorder. In this role, I explored masculine and feminine boundaries, as the personalities were both male and female.

Both shows resonated me, revealing that feelings of isolation are inevitable at some point, but that these feelings can be overcome and life can be positive no matter the circumstances. Furthermore, while the two characters had totally different mind-sets and mannerisms than me, acting in those roles provided vivid life experiences that provided some introspection.

Moreover, this experience has brought me an incredible sense of achievement and no doubt has made me more confident in speaking in front of crowds and discussing issues with colleagues, as we performed to large audiences on the stage of the Mong Man Wai Concert Hall.
